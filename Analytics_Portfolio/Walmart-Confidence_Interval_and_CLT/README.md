# CASE STUDY: Walmart Customer Purchase Behavior

**Title:** How Walmart Can Optimize Marketing & Segmentation Using Customer Spend Data

**Problem:** Unclear impact of demographics on purchase behavior → inefficient targeting & suboptimal marketing ROI

**Metrics:** 
* Average Purchase Value,
* Customer Segments,
* Spend Distribution,
* Confidence Intervals (95%),
* Demographic Trends

**Analysis:** 
* EDA + CLT + Confidence Intervals to compare spending across gender,
* age, and marital status

**Insights:**
* Gender-based spend difference not statistically significant (CI overlap)
* Marital status shows negligible impact on spending, Age group 26–35 = highest spending segment
* Right-skewed distribution → presence of high-value customers

**Business Recommendations:**
* Shift to behavior-based segmentation & personalization to improve ROI and revenue
* Target high-value segments (age 26–35 + high spenders) with tailored campaigns and loyalty programs
* Avoid over-segmentation by gender/marital status; focus on data-driven targeting strategies
